DexGraspNet 2.0: Learning Generative Dexterous Grasping in Large-scale Synthetic Cluttered Scenes

CoRL 2024PKU / Galbotgraspinggenerativesim-to-real

DexGraspNet 2.0 (CoRL 2024, PKU EPIC Lab; Zhang, Liu, Li, Yu, Geng, Ding, Chen, Wang) is a large-scale benchmark and a learning method for generative dexterous grasping in cluttered scenes. It introduces a synthetic dataset of 1,319 objects, 8,270 scenes, and 427 million grasps generated in simulation for the 16-DOF LEAP Hand (and parallel grippers). On top of this data it proposes a two-stage policy that proposes graspable seed points and then generates 6-DOF wrist poses with a diffusion model conditioned on local geometry. The system transfers zero-shot from simulation to the real world via test-time depth restoration, reaching 90.7% real-world dexterous grasping success in cluttered scenes.

Condition grasp generation on LOCAL point-wise geometry rather than global scene features: an ablation conditioning on global features alone collapses to 16.8%/10.9%/4.8% (dense/random/loose) vs the local-feature model's 90.6%/83.7%/73.2%. Two-stage pipeline: (1) Seed Point Proposal — a ResUNet14 (MinkowskiEngine) predicts per-point graspness and object segmentation and extracts local features; (2) Grasp Pose Generation conditioned on those local features. Decompose the high-DOF grasp into a multi-modal wrist pose (translation+rotation as a 12D Euclidean vector, projected to SE(3) via SVD) modeled by a conditional DDPM, plus single-mode finger joint angles predicted by deterministic regression. Build the dataset with two complementary grasp-synthesis optimizers — GWS-based (24.19% per-object success) and force-closure-based (7.91%) — yielding ~1.9M stable grasps per object (190M total) then filtering to ~56K collision-free grasps per scene. Bridge the sim-to-real depth gap with an off-the-shelf test-time depth-restoration step on single-view real depth point clouds, requiring no real-world fine-tuning. Exploit the dexterous hand's envelopment capability to grasp larger objects more effectively than parallel grippers.

A large-scale synthetic cluttered-scene grasping dataset: 1,319 objects, 8,270 scenes, and 427 million dexterous grasps (from ~190M stable per-object grasps). A local-geometry-conditioned generative grasping method that outperforms all baselines in simulation: 90.6%/83.7%/73.2% (dense/random/loose) vs ISAGrasp 63.4%/60.7%/51.4%, GraspTTA 62.5%/54.1%/42.8%, and HGC-Net 46.0%/37.8%/26.7%. Zero-shot sim-to-real transfer achieving a 90.7% real-world dexterous grasping success rate (LEAP Hand) and 92.5% with a parallel gripper, across six iteratively cleared cluttered test scenes. Test-time depth restoration that lifts mixed-object-scene performance from 86.4% to 90.7% (and is unnecessary for purely diffuse scenes, 94.1% either way). Efficient deployment: full inference (seed proposal + grasp generation) runs in under 0.5 s on an NVIDIA RTX 4090, with depth restoration adding only ~0.2 s. Public release of paper, code, and dataset.

Given a single-view depth point cloud of a cluttered tabletop scene, it proposes graspable regions and generates diverse dexterous (LEAP Hand) or parallel-gripper grasp poses, then executes them to clear the clutter. The project page shows synthetic training scenes with generated grasps and eight qualitative real-world execution sequences of dexterous grasping in cluttered scenes.
