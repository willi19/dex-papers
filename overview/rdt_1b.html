RDT-1B: a Diffusion Foundation Model for Bimanual Manipulation

ICLR 2025Tsinghua (THU-ML)diffusionbimanualfoundation

RDT-1B is a 1.2-billion-parameter diffusion-based foundation model for bimanual robotic manipulation, built on a scalable Robotics Diffusion Transformer (RDT) architecture. It targets the two central challenges of bimanual control: the multi-modal action distributions that arise from coordinating two arms, and the scarcity of bimanual training data. To overcome data scarcity it introduces a Physically Interpretable Unified Action Space that lets a single model pre-train across heterogeneous robots and then transfer to a specific bimanual platform. It is presented by its authors as the largest diffusion-based foundation model for robotic manipulation to date.

Uses a diffusion model rather than regression to represent the action distribution, which captures the multi-modal, nonlinear behavior inherent to coordinating two arms. Introduces a Physically Interpretable Unified Action Space so heterogeneous robots can be pooled for pre-training and the model can transfer across different robot embodiments. Scales a Diffusion Transformer with three key modifications: QKNorm & RMSNorm to prevent numerical instability, an MLP decoder (replacing linear projection) to capture nonlinear dynamics, and Alternating Condition Injection (ACI) for cross-attention over image and text tokens. Freezes pre-trained encoders for conditioning: SigLIP as the image-text-aligned vision encoder and T5-XXL as the language encoder. Two-stage recipe: pre-train on a large multi-robot collection, then fine-tune on a self-collected multi-task bimanual dataset. Uses DPM-Solver++ to accelerate sampling (reducing diffusion steps, e.g. 100 to 55), enabling real-time inference.

Pre-trained on the largest multi-robot collection reported, comprising 46 datasets, 1M+ trajectories, and 21TB of data. Fine-tuned on a self-created multi-task bimanual dataset of 6K+ trajectories spanning 300+ tasks, 100+ objects, and 15+ rooms, collected on an ALOHA dual-arm robot. At 1.2B parameters, claimed as the largest diffusion-based foundation model for robotic manipulation. Reports a 56% improvement in success rates across a wide spectrum of challenging tasks over prior methods; e.g., Pour Water (unseen) 62.5% vs ACT 37.5%/Octo 12.5%/OpenVLA 0%, Wash Cup (unseen) 50% vs 0% for all baselines, Instruction Following 87.5% vs OpenVLA 50%, and Fold Shorts (1-shot) 68% vs Octo 4%. Demonstrates few-shot learning of new skills from just 1 to 5 demonstrations and zero-shot generalization to unseen objects and scenes. Achieves real-time inference of 6 action chunks/sec, corresponding to an average of 381 actions/sec.

On an ALOHA dual-arm robot it performs dexterous bimanual tasks such as precise joystick manipulation, pouring water to a commanded level (e.g. 1/3 or 2/3 full), washing cups, and folding shorts, while generalizing to unseen objects and new rooms and following natural-language instructions. The project page shows inference videos covering dexterity, unseen-object and scene generalization, instruction following, few-shot skill acquisition (1-5 demos), and repeated successful executions.
