DexMimicGen: Automated Data Generation for Bimanual Dexterous Manipulation via Imitation Learning

ICRA 2025NVIDIA / UT-Austinimitationdata-generationbimanual

DexMimicGen is a large-scale automated data generation system that synthesizes manipulation trajectories from a handful of human demonstrations for bimanual dexterous robots, including humanoids with multi-fingered hands. It targets the data-acquisition bottleneck in imitation learning, which is especially acute for bimanual dexterous settings where a human must simultaneously control two arms and two dexterous hands. From a small set of teleoperated source demos, the system segments each demonstration into object-centric subtasks and re-composes/transforms them to generate many new successful trajectories across randomized scene configurations in simulation. The resulting synthetic datasets are used to train behavioral cloning policies, and the work also demonstrates a real-to-sim-to-real pipeline on real hardware.

Extends the MimicGen idea of replaying and adapting object-centric subtask segments from a few human demos to the much harder bimanual dexterous / humanoid setting with two arms and multi-fingered hands. Each task is decomposed into subtasks that are transformed to new object poses and stitched together, letting a handful of demos seed thousands of automatically generated trajectories in randomized scenes. Explicitly handles different two-arm coordination requirements, organizing tasks by parallel (independent), coordination (both arms cooperate on one object), and sequential (ordered handoff) structure. Spans three robot embodiments to show generality: bimanual Panda arms with parallel-jaw grippers, bimanual Panda arms with dexterous hands, and the GR-1 humanoid with dexterous (Inspire) hands. Uses the generated data with simple behavioral cloning, and studies how data-generation and policy-learning choices affect performance, finding that policy success improves with larger generated datasets. Includes a real-to-sim-to-real pipeline so policies trained on simulated generated data can be deployed on a real humanoid.

Generates 21K (over 20,000) synthetic demonstrations from just 60 source human demonstrations, an expansion of roughly 350x. Introduces a suite of 9 bimanual dexterous manipulation simulation environments spanning parallel, coordination, and sequential two-arm behaviors (e.g., Threading, Transport, Box/Drawer Cleanup, Tray Lift, Can Sorting, Piece Assembly, Pouring, Coffee). Demonstrates the system across three embodiments: bimanual Panda + parallel-jaw grippers, bimanual Panda + dexterous hands, and the GR-1 humanoid with dexterous hands. Reports high behavioral-cloning success rates on generated data, including Can Sorting 97.3%, Box Cleanup 92.0%, Tray Lift 88.7%, Transport 83.3%, Piece Assembly 80.7%, Pouring 79.3%, Coffee 77.3%, Drawer Cleanup 76.0%, and Threading 69.3%. Presents a real-to-sim-to-real pipeline and deploys it on a real-world humanoid can-sorting task. Provides an empirical study of how data-generation and policy-learning decisions affect agent performance, showing policy performance scales up with larger generated datasets.

It automatically produces large bimanual dexterous manipulation datasets (21K demos from 60 human demos) across 9 simulated tasks such as Can Sorting, Coffee, Pouring, Tray Lift, Box/Drawer Cleanup, Threading, Transport, and Piece Assembly, covering parallel, coordinated, and sequential two-arm behaviors. The generated data trains behavioral cloning policies that are evaluated in simulation and, via a real-to-sim-to-real pipeline, deployed on a real humanoid can-sorting task.
