Sim-to-Real Reinforcement Learning for Vision-Based Dexterous Manipulation on Humanoids

CoRL 2025UC BerkeleyRLsim-to-realbimanual

A practical sim-to-real reinforcement learning recipe (paper by Toru Lin, Kartik Sachdev, Linxi Fan, Jitendra Malik, and Yuke Zhu of UC Berkeley / NVIDIA / UT Austin, arXiv:2502.20396) that trains a multi-fingered humanoid robot to perform vision-based, contact-rich dexterous manipulation. It targets the open problem of extending sim-to-real RL — which has mostly succeeded only in simpler state-based or single-hand setups — to vision-based bimanual tasks. The method is trained entirely in simulation (NVIDIA Isaac Gym) on a Fourier GR1 humanoid with dual multi-fingered hands and deployed zero-shot to the real robot, without human demonstrations or imitation-learning data collection. The authors argue this shows vision-based dexterous manipulation via sim-to-real RL is not only viable but scalable and broadly applicable.

Automated real-to-sim tuning module that searches the parameter space to find optimal simulator-physics and robot-model constants in under four minutes, closing the sim-to-real gap automatically rather than by hand. A generalized, contact-and-object-goal reward formulation using contact-based keypoint markers plus object goal states, avoiding hand-engineered task-specific reward shaping. A divide-and-conquer policy distillation framework plus sample-efficient learning via task-aware hand-pose initialization (under 30 seconds of teleoperation per task). A hybrid object representation strategy that mixes low-dimensional 3D object position (sparse) with high-dimensional depth imagery (dense), each with modality-specific data augmentation. Observations fuse a third-view camera, an egocentric camera, and robot proprioception; domain randomization spans 11+ physical parameters (friction, mass, external forces, PD gains).

Demonstrates a sim-to-real RL recipe on three challenging dexterous tasks — grasp-and-reach, box lift, and bimanual handover — with real-world average success rates of 62.3% (grasp-and-reach), 80% (box lift), and 52.5% (bimanual handover) across all objects, 10 trials per test object. Validates the automated real-to-sim tuning: the lowest-MSE autotuned robot model achieved 8/10 grasp and 7/10 reach success versus 0/10 for the highest-MSE parameters (Table I). Shows the hybrid object representation matters: mixing sparse 3D position with dense depth reached 10/10 pickup success on grasp-and-lift tasks, versus 0/10–2/10 for depth-only across most tasks (Table III). Achieves the automated real-to-sim parameter search in under four minutes, and bootstraps policies with under 30 seconds of teleoperation per task for hand-pose initialization. Generalizes to unseen objects varying in shape, size, material, and mass, and exhibits robustness to external perturbations plus emergent failure-recovery behaviors such as regrasping after object drops — all without human demonstrations.

The trained policies let a bimanual multi-fingered humanoid grasp-and-reach toward target objects, lift a box with two-handed coordination, and hand an object from one hand to the other (bimanual handover). Project-page demo videos show these tasks plus generalization to unseen objects, robustness to force disturbances, and adaptive grasp variation and regrasping recovery from the same policy.
