DexVLA: Vision-Language Model with Plug-In Diffusion Expert for General Robot Control

2025Midea / ECNUVLAdiffusion-expertcross-embodiment

DexVLA is a vision-language-action (VLA) framework for general robot control that pairs a Qwen2-VL 2B vision-language model with a separable, plug-in diffusion-based action expert scaled to one billion parameters. Its central premise is that the action representation (not just the VLM) is the bottleneck for VLAs, so it invests in a large diffusion action head designed for cross-embodiment learning. The system is trained with a novel three-stage embodiment curriculum learning strategy and targets complex, long-horizon tasks across diverse robot embodiments (single-arm, bimanual, and dexterous hand). The authors (Junjie Wen, Yichen Zhu, Jinming Li, Zhibin Tang, Chaomin Shen, Feifei Feng) report it outperforms state-of-the-art baselines including Octo, OpenVLA, and Diffusion Policy.

Scale the action representation, not just the VLM: a diffusion action expert is grown to 1B parameters (built on Scale Diffusion Policy / ScaleDP, a Transformer with 32 layers, hidden size 1280, 16 attention heads) rather than the conventional multi-million-parameter action heads. Plug-in / separable design: the diffusion expert is decoupled from the VLM so it can be pre-trained independently on cross-embodiment data, using a multi-head output architecture where each head corresponds to a specific embodiment. Three-stage embodiment curriculum learning: (1) cross-embodiment pre-training of the diffusion expert alone, (2) embodiment-specific alignment that jointly trains the VLM, projection layer, and diffusion expert (visual encoder frozen), and (3) task-specific adaptation for rapid downstream tuning. Reasoning-conditioned actions: sub-step reasoning annotations (provided about every five seconds) are injected into the policy via FiLM layers that scale and shift the projection-layer parameters, so the model generates reasoning and uses it to guide action. Cross-embodiment generalization with little data, enabling adaptation to novel embodiments and long-horizon tasks using only direct language prompting (e.g., laundry folding) without task-specific architecture changes.

A 1-billion-parameter diffusion action expert; ablation shows it is decisive: shirt folding scores 0.92 with the 1B expert versus 0.17 with a 93M UNet-based action head. Embodiment curriculum learning pre-trained on 100 hours of demonstration data spanning 91 distinct tasks (primary sources Agilex 42.7% and a single Franka 34.7%); Stage 1 (expert-only) trains ~5x faster than full VLA pre-training (0.89 vs 0.32 epoch/hour). Strong zero-extra-adaptation performance: 0.92 on shirt folding where Octo/OpenVLA/Diffusion Policy baselines struggled to complete any steps; outperforms OpenVLA (7B VLM), Octo (93M diffusion), and Diffusion Policy. Fast novel-embodiment learning from only 100 demonstrations: ~0.90 average on dexterous-hand drink pouring and ~0.90 average on bimanual UR5e packing, substantially beating Diffusion Policy. Long-horizon capability via Stage 3: laundry folding 0.4 and dryer unloading 0.8, plus wins on sorting, hard bin picking, and hard table bussing over OpenVLA and Octo. Ablations confirm each design choice: sub-step reasoning is essential (0.07 without Stage 1 reasoning, 0 without any sub-step data) and Stage 1 pre-training is critical (Stage 2-only variants score 0); runs at 60Hz on a single NVIDIA A6000.

DexVLA performs general manipulation across single-arm, bimanual, and dexterous-hand robots, completing complex long-horizon tasks from direct language prompts such as laundry folding, dryer unloading, shirt folding, sorting, bin picking, and table bussing. It also rapidly adapts to novel embodiments and dexterous skills (e.g., dexterous-hand drink pouring, bimanual UR5e packing) from roughly 100 demonstrations.
