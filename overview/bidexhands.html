Towards Human-Level Bimanual Dexterous Manipulation with Reinforcement Learning (Bi-DexHands)

NeurIPS 2022PKU / academicRLbenchmarkbimanual

Bi-DexHands is a benchmark and learning suite for bimanual dexterous manipulation, introduced in the paper "Towards Human-Level Bimanual Dexterous Manipulation with Reinforcement Learning" (Chen et al., NeurIPS 2022 Datasets and Benchmarks Track). It provides two high-DoF dexterous hands (primarily ShadowHand) in NVIDIA Isaac Gym, with tens of bimanual manipulation tasks and thousands of target objects. The tasks are organized to mirror stages of human motor-skill development drawn from cognitive science. The benchmark is GPU-accelerated, reaching 30,000+ FPS (40,000+ mean FPS in the released code on an RTX 3090 with 2,048 parallel environments).

Frame dexterous-manipulation benchmark tasks around human motor-development stages (e.g., skills comparable to those of infants at certain ages) to structure difficulty. Use two cooperating high-DoF dexterous hands, requiring joint control of tens of joints, making the problem naturally suited to both single-agent and multi-agent RL formulations. Leverage massively parallel GPU simulation in Isaac Gym to make large-scale RL training tractable on a single consumer GPU. Expose a unified Gym-like API supporting a broad spectrum of learning paradigms: single-agent RL, multi-agent RL, offline RL, multi-task RL, and meta RL. Provide thousands of object variations so that generalization, multi-task, and few-shot learning can be studied, not just single-task mastery.

A bimanual dexterous-hand benchmark with tens of manipulation tasks and thousands of target objects (16+ environments in the released code, e.g., Catch, Over, Lift, Door, Bottle cap, Scissors, Block stack, Re-orientation, Pour water). GPU-accelerated simulation reaching 30,000+ FPS on a single NVIDIA RTX 3090 (40,000+ mean FPS reported in the codebase running 2,048 parallel environments). Comprehensive algorithm support: single-agent RL (PPO, TRPO, TD3, SAC, DDPG), multi-agent RL (HAPPO, HATRPO, MAPPO, IPPO, MADDPG), and multi-task/meta RL (MTPPO, MTTRPO, MTSAC, ProMP). Empirical finding that PPO masters simpler tasks (e.g., catching, bottle-opening) while multi-agent RL handles complex bimanual coordination (e.g., pot-lifting, block-stacking). Identification of open challenges: existing methods struggle with multi-task and few-shot/generalization settings, motivating future research. Open-source release with point-cloud/visual observation support, offline-RL datasets, and rl-games compatibility.

It demonstrates two dexterous hands learning a wide range of bimanual skills in simulation, including catching, bottle/door opening, scissors and pen-cap use, pouring water, and block stacking. Single-agent RL solves the simpler tasks while multi-agent RL is used for the harder cooperative coordination tasks.
