ManipTrans: Efficient Dexterous Bimanual Manipulation Transfer via Residual Learning

CVPR 2025BIGAI (State Key Laboratory of General Artificial Intelligence), Tsinghua University, Peking UniversityRLimitation-learningsim-to-realbimanual

ManipTrans is a method for efficiently transferring human bimanual manipulation skills to dexterous robotic hands in physics simulation. It first pre-trains a generalist trajectory imitator that mimics human hand motion, then fine-tunes a task-specific residual module under physics-based interaction constraints. This decoupling of motion imitation from physical effects enables fast, accurate execution of complex bimanual tasks. The approach is used to retarget multiple hand-object datasets into a new robot dataset, DexManipNet.

Two-stage design: a generalist hand-trajectory imitator is pre-trained to follow human hand motion, then a per-task residual module is fine-tuned on top of it. The residual module corrects for physical interaction effects (contact, object dynamics) rather than re-learning motion from scratch, improving sample efficiency. Decouples pure motion imitation from interaction/contact handling, so the same imitator backbone generalizes across tasks and embodiments. Generalizes across multiple robot hands of differing morphology and DoF (Inspire 12-DoF, Shadow 22-DoF, articulated MANO 22-DoF, Allegro 16-DoF). Used to build DexManipNet, a large-scale physically-consistent robot manipulation dataset including previously unexplored bimanual tasks such as pen capping and bottle unscrewing. Demonstrated with real-world deployment using custom hardware (90-degree flange with direct dexterous-hand mounting).

Introduces ManipTrans, a two-stage residual-learning method for transferring human bimanual skills to dexterous robot hands in simulation. Releases DexManipNet: 3.3K episodes / 1.34 million frames across 61 tasks and 1.2K objects, ~600 of them complex bimanual sequences. Achieves 58.1% (single-hand) / 39.5% (bimanual) success rate, outperforming RL-Only (34.3% / 12.1%) and Retarget+Residual (47.8% / 13.9%) baselines. Reports low tracking error: 8.60 degrees object rotation error, 0.49 cm object translation error, 2.15 cm mean per-joint position error, and 1.36 cm mean per-fingertip position error. Trains a single-hand task in ~15 minutes versus QuasiSim's ~40 hours, a large efficiency gain. Validates the learned policies on real hardware and on multiple robot hand embodiments (Inspire, Shadow, MANO, Allegro).

Given human bimanual hand-object motion data, it produces physically valid dexterous robot-hand policies that reproduce the manipulation in simulation and on real hardware, enabling complex bimanual tasks like pen capping and bottle unscrewing.
