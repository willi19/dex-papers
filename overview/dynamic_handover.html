Dynamic Handover: Throw and Catch with Bimanual Hands

CoRL 2023UC San Diego and Peking University (Xiaolong Wang group)RLmulti-agent-RLsim-to-realbimanual

Dynamic Handover studies high-speed, dynamic object handover performed by a system of two multi-finger dexterous hands mounted on robot arms. One hand throws an object and the other catches it in mid-air, requiring fast, precise collaboration between the two arm-hand agents. The policy is trained with Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) in simulation and deployed on physical robots through sim-to-real transfer. To close the sim-to-real gap, the authors introduce algorithm designs including a learned object trajectory prediction model. The work targets a regime of dexterous manipulation previously dominated by human performers.

Formulates the throw-and-catch handover as a multi-agent problem, with the throwing hand and catching hand trained jointly via Multi-Agent RL. Trains entirely in simulation and transfers to real hardware using sim-to-real techniques to bridge the dynamics gap. Learns a trajectory prediction model that estimates in real time where the in-flight object is heading, letting the catcher position itself ahead of the object. Targets high-speed, dynamic actions rather than slow quasi-static handovers, demanding precise coordination and timing between the two hands. Evaluates on a diversity of objects, including basic geometric shapes and real-life objects, plus perturbation and failure-case analyses.

A bimanual dexterous system of two multi-finger hands that throws and catches objects, trained with Multi-Agent RL in simulation. A sim-to-real pipeline that transfers the learned throw-and-catch policy to real robots. A learned object trajectory prediction model enabling real-time estimation of the object's flight path for the catching hand. Real-world evaluation across multiple objects (basic geometric and real-life objects) showing improvements over multiple baselines, including multi-agent vs. alternative baselines. Robustness studies including perturbation tests (orthogonal, opposing, along-direction) and failure-case analyses.

It enables two arm-mounted multi-finger robot hands to dynamically throw an object with one hand and catch it in mid-air with the other, generalizing across a variety of objects in the real world.
