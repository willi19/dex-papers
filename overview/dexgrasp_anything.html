DexGrasp Anything: Towards Universal Robotic Dexterous Grasping with Physics Awareness

CVPR 2025 (Highlight)ShanghaiTech University (4DVLab) — Yiming Zhong, Qi Jiang, Jingyi Yu, Yuexin Madiffusiongraspingdexterousdataset

DexGrasp Anything is a generative method for synthesizing dexterous-hand grasp poses on diverse objects. It integrates physical constraints into both the training and sampling phases of a diffusion-based generative model. Geometric point-cloud features (Point Transformer) are enriched with LLM-generated semantic descriptions of object shape and grasping properties (encoded via BERT-large) and fused through cross-attention. The authors also release a new dataset and target the Shadow Hand.

Physics-aware training: clean grasp-pose estimates derived from noisy diffusion inputs are constrained by physical objectives during training. Physics-Guided Sampler: posterior refinement iteratively steers grasp configurations toward physically feasible solutions during sampling. Three physical forces guide generation — Surface Pulling Force (finger surfaces toward object), External-Penetration Repulsion (avoid hand-object collisions via signed distance), and Self-Penetration Repulsion (preserve hand geometry). LLM-based semantic conditioning: an LLM describes object shape/grasp properties, processed through BERT-large and merged with geometric features via cross-attention. Built on a diffusion generative backbone conditioned on object point clouds encoded by a Point Transformer.

A physics-aware diffusion grasp generator embedding three physical constraints across both training and sampling phases. A new dexterous grasping dataset with over 3.4 million diverse grasping poses across 15,698 objects (combining curated sources and model-generated data) — described as the largest publicly available dataset for the Shadow Hand. State-of-the-art results across five benchmarks (DexGraspNet, UniDexGrasp, MultiDex, RealDex, DexGRAB), surpassing prior dexterous grasp-generation methods on most metrics. On DexGraspNet, 57.5 (6-direction) and 90.6 (1-direction) success rate with LLM enhancement, vs 53.6 and 90.4 without LLM. Real-world robot experiments demonstrating stable post-grasp motion using both manual control and IK-based motion planning.

Given an object point cloud, it generates physically feasible dexterous (Shadow-hand) grasp poses for arbitrary objects, achieving high grasp success and diversity across multiple datasets.
