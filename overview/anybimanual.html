AnyBimanual: Transferring Unimanual Policy for General Bimanual Manipulation

ICCV 2025Tsinghua University (Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School) & Nanyang Technological Universityimitation-learningmanipulationbimanualsim-to-real

AnyBimanual is a model-agnostic method for adapting arbitrary pre-trained single-arm (unimanual) manipulation policies to two-armed (bimanual) tasks. It avoids costly from-scratch bimanual training by reusing sharable unimanual skills, requiring only a small amount of bimanual data. The framework combines a skill manager that dynamically schedules learned skill primitives and a visual aligner that bridges the visual domain gap between unimanual pretraining and bimanual deployment. It is validated on both simulated (RLBench2) and real-world bimanual tasks.

Skill manager: decomposes pretrained unimanual policy knowledge into learnable skill primitives, combined via importance weights plus task-oriented compensation to generate arm-specific instructions. Visual aligner: generates spatial soft masks to decompose voxel observations per arm, mitigating the visual domain shift between unimanual pretraining and bimanual deployment. Model-agnostic, plug-and-play design that can transfer arbitrary pre-trained unimanual policies to bimanual policies. Leverages sharable unimanual skills to orchestrate coordinated two-arm manipulation with only a few bimanual demonstrations. Demonstrates generalization to unseen colors, backgrounds, shapes, and sizes.

A model-agnostic plug-and-play framework that transfers pre-trained unimanual policies into generalizable bimanual policies with few bimanual demonstrations. A skill manager for dynamic scheduling of skill representations discovered from the unimanual policy. A visual aligner using spatial soft masking to reduce the observation discrepancy between unimanual pretraining and bimanual deployment. Simulation: 12.67% average success-rate improvement over the prior method PerAct2 across 12 RLBench2 bimanual tasks (100 demonstrations). Real-world: 84.62% average success rate across 9 real-world bimanual tasks (65 test episodes).

It converts pretrained one-arm robot manipulation policies into coordinated two-arm policies, so a bimanual system can perform tasks like handovers, lifting, folding cloth, and washing dishes without training a bimanual policy from scratch.
