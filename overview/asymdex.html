AsymDex: Asymmetry and Relative Coordinates for RL-based Bimanual Dexterity

WCBM @ RSS 2025 (Spotlight); arXiv 2411.13020 (Nov 2024)STAR Lab, Georgia Institute of TechnologyRLbimanualdexteroussim-to-real

AsymDex (Asymmetric Dexterity) is a simple reinforcement learning framework for learning a broad class of bimanual skills with multi-fingered hands, without relying on human demonstrations. It builds on two insights inspired by human handedness: one hand acts as a 'facilitating' hand that repositions and reorients an object while the other acts as a 'dominant' hand that performs the fine manipulation. It defines observation and action spaces in relative coordinates (a frame attached to the object held by the facilitating hand), which captures hand-to-hand coordination directly. The framework integrates with grasp learning to form a full two-phase pipeline (grasp acquisition followed by interaction). It is evaluated on seven asymmetric bimanual tasks (four simulated, three real-world) using dual ShadowHands.

Asymmetry: assign complementary roles to each hand — a facilitating hand restricted to base motion (no finger control) and a fully-controlled dominant hand — mirroring human handedness. Relative coordinates: transform states and actions into a frame attached to the object held by the facilitating hand, enabling time-independent responsive coordination. Dimensionality reduction from these two ideas: observation space cut from 176 to 88 dims and action space from 52 to 26 dims. Two-phase decomposition: a grasp-acquisition phase integrated with a downstream interaction/coordination phase rather than one monolithic policy. Demonstration-free: learns purely via RL with no teleoperation or expert trajectories. Ablations show both asymmetry and relative motion are needed; relative coordinates give the larger individual gain but the combination is essential.

First RL-based approach for asymmetric bimanual dexterity with multi-fingered hands that requires no demonstrations. Interaction-phase success rates substantially beat baselines: Block-in-cup 77.01% (vs 4.29% monolithic), Switch 67.00% (vs 5.63%), Bottle-cap 83.01% (vs 61.25%); Stack 92.33% (vs 25.17% monolithic, comparable to 94.43% rel-only). Full grasp+coordination pipeline (2-stage AsymDex) reaches Block-in-cup 79.38% (vs 3.21% 1-stage and 15.05% 2-stage monolithic) and Bottle-cap 87.26% (vs 16.80% and 64.07%). Evaluated across seven asymmetric bimanual tasks: four simulated (block-in-cup, stack cups, bottle-cap removal, switch) and three real-world (block-in-cup, pour, twist-lid) on dual 30-DoF ShadowHands. Improved sample efficiency as well as final success rate relative to monolithic and single-insight (asymmetry-only / relative-only) baselines.

Trains RL policies that let a two-handed multi-fingered robot perform coordinated bimanual manipulation tasks — placing a block in a cup, pouring/stacking, removing a bottle cap, and activating a switch — by having one hand stabilize an object while the other manipulates it, transferring to real-world ShadowHands.
