EgoDex: Learning Dexterous Manipulation from Large-Scale Egocentric Video

ICLR 2026Appleimitation-learninghuman-videodatasetdiffusion

EgoDex is a large-scale egocentric video dataset for dexterous manipulation, collected with Apple Vision Pro. It comprises 829 hours of 1080p/30fps egocentric video paired with synchronized 3D hand and upper-body pose annotations captured at recording time via the device's calibrated cameras and on-device SLAM. The dataset spans 194 different tabletop manipulation tasks with everyday household objects, ranging from tying shoelaces to folding laundry. Alongside the data, the authors train and benchmark imitation-learning policies for hand trajectory prediction and introduce evaluation metrics for the domain.

Use Apple Vision Pro to passively capture human manipulation: 829 hours of egocentric 1080p/30fps video with native, simultaneously recorded 3D skeletal hand/upper-body pose (25 joints per hand at 30 Hz) rather than post-processed estimates. Egocentric video collection scales without active robot teleoperation hardware, enabling far larger and more diverse data. Action representation of 48 dims per timestep: 2 hands x (3 wrist position + 6D wrist orientation + 3D x 5 fingertips). Two benchmark tasks: future hand trajectory prediction from observations/poses/language, and inverse dynamics (visually goal-conditioned hand motion to a goal image). Systematically compare six model variants (encoder-decoder vs decoder-only Transformer x behavior cloning / diffusion / flow matching), using best-of-K sampling to handle motion multimodality.

Releases EgoDex: 829 hours of egocentric video, 338,000 task demonstrations, ~90 million frames, 2.0 TB compressed, across 194 tasks. Provides paired 3D pose for all upper-body joints including 25 joints per hand at 30 Hz, recorded natively at collection time. Roughly tenfold larger than the next-comparable dexterous manipulation dataset, with broader task coverage beyond pick-and-place. Introduces two reproducible benchmarks (trajectory prediction and inverse dynamics) with formal train/test splits and Euclidean-distance metrics. Reports that encoder-decoder beats decoder-only, flow matching is 34% better than behavior cloning at K=10, and visual goal-conditioning cuts average distance 22% and final distance 53%; performance scales with dataset size. Publicly available for download at github.com/apple/ml-egodex.

It supplies a large-scale, hand-pose-annotated egocentric video dataset and trains imitation-learning policies that predict future dexterous hand trajectories (and goal-conditioned hand motion) from video, language, and pose.
