ALOHA Unleashed: A Simple Recipe for Robot Dexterity

CoRL 2024Google DeepMindimitation-learningdiffusionbimanualteleoperation

ALOHA Unleashed is an imitation-learning recipe for dexterous bimanual manipulation on the ALOHA 2 platform. It pairs a scalable human teleoperation pipeline for collecting large amounts of demonstration data with an expressive Transformer encoder-decoder policy trained with a diffusion loss. The work asks how far pure imitation learning can be pushed on highly dexterous, contact-rich tasks. It is evaluated on 5 challenging real-world tasks and 3 simulated tasks, outperforming prior state-of-the-art baselines.

A simple recipe: scale up human teleoperation data collection on ALOHA 2, then train an expressive diffusion-based policy on it. Transformer encoder-decoder architecture (217M parameters: 85M encoder, 55M decoder) with a diffusion action head, using a ResNet50 backbone over four 480x640 RGB camera views. Action chunking: the policy outputs 50-step action chunks (~1 second of control) over 14 DOF (12 joint positions + 2 gripper values). Diffusion Policy provides the expressiveness needed to model multimodal, high-precision bimanual behaviors that simpler regression policies struggle with. Demonstrates that imitation learning alone, given enough data and a capable model, can solve tasks like knot-tying and self-repair without RL or task-specific engineering.

Collected 26,000+ teleoperated demonstration episodes across 10 robots over 8 months for the real-world tasks. Shirt hanging: 8,658 demos, 75% success (easy) / 70% (messy initialization). Shoelace tying: 5,133 demos, 70% success (easy) / 40% (messy). Robot finger replacement: 5,247 demos, 75% success; gear insertion: 4,005 demos, 95% (1 gear) / 75% (2 gears) / 40% (3 gears). Random kitchen stacking: 3,198 demos, 95% (bowl only) / 65% (bowl+cup) / 25% (bowl+cup+fork). Trained for 2M steps on 64 TPUv5e chips with ~0.043s inference per decision; validated on 3 simulated tasks (single/double peg insertion, mug-on-plate) beating prior baselines.

It runs autonomous policies on 5 dexterous real-world bimanual tasks: hanging a shirt on a hanger, tying shoelaces, replacing a worn robot finger, inserting gears onto pegs, and stacking randomly initialized kitchen items. Demo videos for all real and simulated tasks are shown on the project page.
