SAM 3: Segment Anything with Concepts

arXiv 2025 (Nov 2025; v2 Mar 2026)Meta AI (FAIR)segmentationfoundationopen-vocabularyvideo

SAM 3 is the successor to SAM and SAM 2, a single unified model from Meta AI (FAIR) that detects, segments, and tracks objects in both images and videos. Its central new capability is Promptable Concept Segmentation (PCS): instead of only point/box/mask clicks, it accepts concept prompts defined as short noun phrases (e.g. 'yellow school bus'), image exemplars, or a combination of both. Architecturally it pairs an image-level detector with a memory-based video tracker that share a single backbone, and adds a 'presence head' that decouples recognition (is the concept present?) from localization (where is it?) to improve detection accuracy. It still supports the original SAM-style visual prompts (points, boxes, masks).

Promptable Concept Segmentation (PCS): segment all instances of a concept given a short noun phrase, an image exemplar, or both. Unified image detector plus memory-based video tracker sharing one backbone for consistent image and video segmentation/tracking. A 'presence head' decouples recognition from localization, improving detection accuracy. A scalable data engine builds a large, high-quality dataset including explicit hard negatives. Retains classic SAM visual prompting (points, boxes, masks) alongside the new concept prompts.

Introduces Promptable Concept Segmentation (PCS) for open-vocabulary text/exemplar-driven segmentation in images and video. Roughly 2x gain over existing systems on both image and video PCS (SA-Co benchmark); humans prefer SAM 3 outputs over the strongest baseline ~3 to 1. Built a dataset with ~4 million unique concept labels (including hard negatives) across images and videos via a scalable data engine. Runs in ~30 ms for a single image with 100+ detected objects on an H200 GPU; the SAM 3.1 update reaches ~32 FPS on a single H100 and tracks up to 16 objects in one forward pass. Open-sources SAM 3 together with the new Segment Anything with Concepts (SA-Co) benchmark for promptable concept segmentation.

Given a text noun phrase, an image exemplar, or visual prompts (points/boxes/masks), it segments and tracks all matching object instances across images and video, including diverse and first-person/egocentric footage. Demos cover open-vocabulary 'segment everything called X' queries with consistent tracking through video frames.
