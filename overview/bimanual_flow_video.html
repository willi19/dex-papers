Towards a Generalizable Bimanual Foundation Policy via Flow-based Video Prediction

arXiv preprint (2505.24156, May 2025)Institute of Artificial Intelligence (TeleAI), China Telecom; Northwestern Polytechnical University; Hong Kong University of Science and TechnologyVLAdiffusionhuman-videobimanual

CogRobot is a foundation policy for bimanual robot manipulation that repurposes a pretrained text-to-video model (CogVideoX-2B) as a high-level visual planner. Instead of predicting video directly from language, it decomposes generation into two stages connected by optical flow: a text-to-flow model that concretizes the language instruction into a motion field, and a flow-to-video model that uses that flow as guidance for fine-grained video prediction. A lightweight diffusion policy then extracts executable joint actions from the predicted video. The optical-flow intermediate reduces reliance on large bimanual datasets and mitigates physical hallucination, task confusion, and instruction ambiguity seen in single-stage video prediction.

Use optical flow as an intermediate variable, decomposing video generation into text-to-flow and flow-to-video stages, both built on CogVideoX. Fine-tune a pretrained text-to-video foundation model rather than training from scratch, lowering data requirements for bimanual tasks. Text-to-flow model predicts a motion field (rendered as RGB flow video) to disambiguate the language instruction before pixel-level prediction. Flow-to-video model concatenates flow and RGB latents so predicted flow guides fine-grained future-frame generation. A lightweight diffusion policy converts the generated video into executable dual-arm joint actions. Built a custom dual-arm platform (two Realman 7-DoF arms, single-view RealSense RGB) with VR-based teleoperation for data collection.

A framework for training bimanual foundation policies by fine-tuning text-to-video models, avoiding training from scratch. A two-stage text-to-flow then flow-to-video paradigm using optical flow as guidance to cut data requirements. A custom dual-arm hardware/data platform (two Realman 7-DoF arms) with VR-based teleoperation; evaluated on 4 simulation tasks and 2 real-world tasks with 100 demonstrations each. Simulation (RoboTwin): 100% success on 'Put Apple Cabinet' vs. 82% for the Diffusion Policy baseline. Real-world: 70% success on 'Lift Bag' (vs. 50% for DP) and 75% on 'Pull Box' (vs. 5% for DP). Video-quality gains over direct fine-tuning on RoboMIND: PSNR +2.986, SSIM +0.052, LPIPS -0.054, FVD -488.

Given a language instruction and a single RGB observation, CogRobot predicts an optical-flow-guided future video of a bimanual task and extracts dual-arm joint actions from it, generalizing across simulated and real-world manipulation tasks.
