DemoStart: Demonstration-led Auto-curriculum Applied to Sim-to-Real with Multi-fingered Robots

2024Google DeepMindRLauto-curriculumsim-to-real

DemoStart is an auto-curriculum reinforcement learning method from Google DeepMind for learning complex multi-fingered manipulation behaviors from only a sparse reward and a handful of demonstrations collected in simulation. It trains a privileged-information policy in simulation and distills it into a vision-based policy (raw multi-camera RGB pixels plus proprioception) that transfers zero-shot to a real robot via domain randomization. The robot platform is a 7-DoF Kuka LBR iiwa 14 arm equipped with the 12-DoF DEX-EE three-fingered dexterous hand, giving an 18-dimensional action space (6D Cartesian arm velocity plus 12D finger joint positions). The central claim is that learning in simulation with this curriculum outperforms policies learned from real-robot demonstrations while requiring roughly 100x fewer demonstrations.

Auto-curriculum from demonstrations: states drawn from a handful of demonstrations are converted into reset/initialization tasks of varying difficulty, building an automatic curriculum from easy (near-success) to hard (full task) starting points. Zero-Variance Filtering (ZVF): the curriculum preferentially selects task parameters where the current policy sometimes succeeds and sometimes fails, concentrating training on states that yield the strongest learning signal. Demonstration state bias: earlier demonstration states are prioritized to keep the policy in natural configurations and avoid learning from unnatural/out-of-distribution poses. Two-stage train-then-distill pipeline: a privileged-information policy is trained in simulation, then distilled into a vision-based (multi-camera pixel + proprioception) policy for deployment. Zero-shot sim-to-real via domain randomization: policies transfer directly to the real DEX-EE hand without real-robot fine-tuning, handling complex contact-rich dynamics. Works from only sparse rewards plus a few sub-optimal demonstrations, removing the need for dense reward shaping or large teleoperation datasets.

Achieves >98% success across all simulated tasks: plug insertion 99.6%, plug lifting 99.7%, cube reorientation 99.9%, nut-and-bolt threading 99.8%, and screwdriver-in-cup 98.6%. Requires ~100x fewer demonstrations than real-robot teleoperation learning — e.g. cube reorientation 2 demos, plug lifting 5, plug insertion 20, nut-and-bolt threading 60, versus 2,000+ real teleoperation episodes for comparable approaches. Demonstrates zero-shot sim-to-real transfer on a multi-fingered hand: plug lifting 97%, cube reorientation 97%, and plug insertion 64% (the task with the largest sim-to-real domain gap). Outperforms both vanilla RL and policies learned from real-robot demonstrations on the transferred tasks. Dramatically reduces demonstration-collection effort: simulated demos took ~30 minutes versus ~27 hours of human teleoperation on the real robot for plug insertion. Learns directly from raw multi-camera pixels (4 RGB cameras) plus proprioception, avoiding hand-engineered state estimation.

DemoStart solves contact-rich dexterous tasks on a three-fingered hand including cube reorientation, plug lifting, plug insertion, nut-and-bolt threading, and inserting a screwdriver into a cup. The project page shows simulation videos (with domain randomization and object perturbations) and real-robot demonstrations comparing DemoStart against baselines.
