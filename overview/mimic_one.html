mimic-one: a Scalable Model Recipe for General Purpose Robot Dexterity

arXiv 2506.11916 (June 2025)mimic robotics & ETH Zurich (ETH AI Center, Soft Robotics Lab, Institute of Neuroinformatics)diffusionimitation-learningteleoperationdexterous

mimic-one is an end-to-end recipe for learning real-world control of a highly dexterous humanoid robotic hand. It pairs a newly designed 16-DoF tendon-driven hand (soft contact skin, wide-angle wrist cameras) mounted on a Franka Emika Panda arm with a diffusion-based visuomotor policy. Demonstrations are collected via a scalable teleoperation pipeline using both glove-based and Apple Vision Pro VR interfaces. The work emphasizes sample efficiency, smooth fine-motor action inference, and a data-collection protocol designed to surface emergent self-correcting behaviors.

UNet Diffusion Policy with CLIP ViT-B/16 image encoders and linear projections for proprioception. Action representation mixes relative-frame Cartesian end-effector targets (6D rotation) with absolute hand joint angles. Long action chunks of 48 timesteps (~3.2s at 15 Hz) with a 2-step observation horizon and 16-step DDIM sampling. Data-collection protocol uses systematic variation, targeted curation, and deliberate self-correction trajectories. Novel 16-DoF tendon-driven hand with soft skin and wrist cameras, biomechanically inspired. Dual teleoperation interfaces (glove + Apple Vision Pro hand/wrist tracking) for scalable demonstration capture.

Diffusion-policy recipe reaching up to 93.3% out-of-distribution success (Bread Pick on full dataset). Emergent self-correcting behaviors yielding up to +33.3% performance boost (e.g. Bottle Sort). Demonstrated scaling trends, e.g. Bread Pick rising from 22.5% at 20% data to 93.3% at 100% data. Dataset of ~5,442 episodes across three task families (bread pick, bottle sort, battery insertion), filtered to successful subsets. Novel 16-DoF tendon-driven dexterous hand with soft contact skin and wide-angle wrist cameras. Scalable glove + Apple Vision Pro teleoperation system and curated data-collection protocol.

Trains a diffusion policy that autonomously performs dexterous real-world tasks (bread roll pick-and-place, bottle sorting/insertion, precise battery insertion) on a 16-DoF humanoid hand, generalizing to out-of-distribution conditions and self-correcting mid-task.
